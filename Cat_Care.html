Cat Care

Feeding

  • By the time kittens are five to six weeks old, they should be nibbling on a high-quality dry food consistently
  • Kittens can be fed free-choice—which means food is available at all times
  • As a general rule of thumb, we recommend that all cats be fed twice daily using the portion control feeding method
  • Milk should not be fed to cats as a treat or a substitute for water.
  • Treats should be no more than 5-10% of the diet.
List from ASPCA

Housing

For your cats housing, it should have its own clean and dry place in your room to sleep and rest. Maybe a cat bed, a bunch of warm blankets or towels. Most of the time cats pick their own place to sleep around your room so just make sure that it has a space.

Scratching

Cats need to scratch, because “when a cat scratches, the old outer nail sheath is pulled off and the sharp, smooth claws underneath are exposed”ASPCA. For your cat not to use your couch as a scratcher. Make sure to provide a scratching post or a scratching pad.

Cutting their nails should be done every two to three weeks to make sure they're not too sharp. We don't want anyone getting injured.
